What happens behind the scenes

Here’s the lifecycle from a scheduled job to verified restore results.

Designed for reliability

Scheduler triggers backup

Your configured schedule runs the agent automatically.

MySQL dump is created

A consistent backup artifact is generated for the configured database(s).

Backup is encrypted locally

Encryption happens before upload to reduce risk in transit and storage.

Upload to your S3 bucket

The encrypted backup is uploaded under a prefix you control.

Verification restores (scheduled)

A restore test runs on a cadence and records pass/fail outcomes.

Portal updates

Backup history and verification results appear in your client portal for visibility and audits.

Step-by-step

A clear sequence you can share with your team.

1) Install

Install the agent on the MySQL server (or a trusted runner) and add your configuration.

Typical time: 10–20 minutes

2) Backup

The agent creates a MySQL backup, encrypts it locally, and uploads it to your S3 bucket.

Runs on your schedule

3) Verify

Verification runs scheduled test restores and records results, giving you proof of recovery.

Ideal cadence: weekly or nightly
